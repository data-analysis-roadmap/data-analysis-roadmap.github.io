Introduction

Throughout 2021-2022 the Castleman Disease Collaborative Network (CDCN) has conducted a survey of US-based rare disease non profit organizations (RDNPs) and their stakeholder populations (patients, loved ones, physicians and researchers).

We had 147 organizations participate, and along with their stakeholders we received over 1,900 survey responses.

In the following report, we would like to present a high-level overview of the findings from our data to help provide answers to some fundamental questions regarding the state of rare disease drug repurposing in the US.

ROADMAP survey

The CDCN reached out to 755 US-based rare disease non-profit organizations on 9/29/2021, and throughout the next two months 147 (19.4%) organizations participated in the survey. _____/% of these also forwarded the survey on to their patient, loved one, physician and researcher communities.

Stakeholder group representation among RDNP leadership

Interestingly, more than 65% of the rare disease organization representatives themselves are also representatives of these other stakeholder groups.

Most of these (67, 25%) are also rare disease patient's loved ones, which include parents, spouses, siblings, etc.

Rare diseases of focus

Generally, all 147 organizations who participated in the survey focused on either a unique rare disease or set of diseases. Only Usher Syndrome and Neurofibromatosis had two organizations each in our dataset.
